# Problem Definition & Use-Case Selection Pathway

The step-by-step we'll fill in live on Thursday. Based on the **Opportunity Solution Tree (OST)**: map the desired outcome → opportunities (needs) → solutions → experiments, and reduce unknowns by testing, not building.

---

## Step 1 — Problem statement (fill one, only one)

> For **[target customer]**, who is trying to **[job/goal]**, the problem is **[friction]**, which today they solve by **[current workaround]**. This costs them **[time / money / risk]**. We'll know it's real when **[observable signal]**.

**Bad smell check:** if the sentence names a feature ("a widget that…"), it's a solution, not a problem. Rewrite.

---

## Step 6 — Revenue hypothesis to test

Pick the model to probe first for the chosen use case:

- **Direct-pay (B2C/B2B):** user pays to remove a specific operational friction. *Who pays? For what?*
- **Freemium:** core is free; gate advanced features or distribution behind the paywall. *What's worth gating?*
- **Sponsor-supported widget:** community members pay nothing; a sponsor funds it. *Who sponsors and why?*
