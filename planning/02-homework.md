# First Steps & Homework — Get the Data to Define the Problem

## What we're actually stuck on

We can't pick the problem because we're arguing from opinion. Three hunches don't break a tie — **evidence does.** So the entire job before Thursday is to replace "I think HOAs feel X" with "here's what N of them actually said/did."

**The homework is not to form a view. It's to go get the smallest pile of real data that makes the problem obvious.**

> If Thursday starts and no one has talked to a potential customer or pulled a real data point, we will have another opinion meeting. That's the failure mode we're avoiding.

---

## The decision we have to make Thursday
> **For which customer is which problem painful enough, frequent enough, and reachable enough that they'd pay to fix it?**

Everything below exists to feed that one sentence with facts.

---

## Before Thursday — everyone runs discovery, not prep

### The shared target: 5–6 real data points by Thursday
Split so no one has to do all of it. Raw notes beat polished summaries — bring the actual quotes.

### Tejas — get us in the room
1. **Do 2 discovery calls** with HOA managers/board members (or the closest real person you can reach). Not a pitch — 20 min of "walk me through the last time X was a pain." Bring **verbatim notes**: what they said hurt, what they currently pay for or hack around, how often it happens.
2. **Prove reachability.** List every HOA contact you can actually get on a call this week. If you can't reach 5, that's itself a finding — say so, so we don't pick a customer we can't validate.
3. Note any moment on a call where someone said *"I'd pay for that"* or *"I already pay for…"* — willingness-to-pay evidence is gold.

### Jason — pull the signal that already exists
1. **Do 1 discovery call** (spread the load; different contact than Tejas).
2. **Mine what we already have:** waitlist, past conversations, inbound interest, anything with a name attached. Which problem do people already gravitate to unprompted?
3. **Competitor/substitute scan:** what do HOAs (or our alt candidate) pay for today, and what does it cost? Existing spend is the hardest evidence of real pain.

### Odi — make the data collectable and comparable
1. ✅ This pack.
2. **Ship the interview guide + the discovery mini-lesson** *before* the calls, not after — so Tejas and Jason's calls actually produce comparable data and don't lead the witness. (This is the "Teach Discovery" item, moved earlier because the calls depend on it.)
3. **Set up a one-page evidence log** everyone drops findings into, so Thursday we read one sheet instead of three memories.

---

## What each data point must capture (drop into the evidence log)

| Field | Why it decides the problem |
|-------|----------------------------|
| Who they are (role, customer type) | Tests reachability + whether they're the buyer |
| The pain, in their words | Keeps us honest — no paraphrasing into our solution |
| How often it hits | Frequency = one of our scoring axes |
| What they do about it today | Reveals real cost + whether a substitute already exists |
| Any $ signal (pay today / would pay) | Willingness to pay, the axis opinions can't fake |

Five of these across the leading candidate and one alternative is enough to score the grid in [01](./01-problem-definition-pathway.md) with data instead of guesses.

## Thursday becomes a read-out, not a debate
1. Dump the evidence log on the board.
2. Score the candidates from the data (not from feelings).
3. The problem statement writes itself from the highest-scoring, best-evidenced row.
4. Circle the assumptions the data *didn't* answer → those are next week's interviews + the pilot.
