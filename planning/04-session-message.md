Hey Tejas, Jason,

Locking in our whiteboard session for **Thursday at 4 PM**. Really looking forward to sitting down together on this. Here's what we're trying to get out of it, how we'll run it, and what to bring.

**Goals: we leave with two decisions, nothing more.**
1. **The issue:** the one problem space we commit to. We're basing it on something we can actually feel, which is what people hate about group collaboration, and where that pain bites hardest.
2. **A use case within the issue:** the single concrete situation (one actor, one job) we'll build and test first.

Everything else, meaning features, revenue model, roadmap, waits until those two are locked.

**Agenda (~90 min):**
- **Warm-up (10):** put our own group-collaboration frustrations on the board, plus what a *good* group project looks like. The issue is the gap between them.
- **Define the issue (30):** cluster the frictions, pick the sharpest one and who feels it most, write one issue statement, and run it through four tests: is it real, ours, recurring, big enough.
- **Decide the use case (35):** list 4 to 6 candidate use cases inside the issue, score them (pain, testability, reachability, path to revenue, buildable by Dec), pick one, name its riskiest assumption.
- **First test and next steps (15):** define the smallest test for that assumption and who owns what.

**Homework (do it together before Thursday):**
1. Write down 10 to 15 things we hate about group collaboration, specific real moments, plus the flip side: what's true when a group project *works*.
2. Have **3 short conversations** with people who run group efforts (HOA/community first). Not a pitch. Ask them to walk you through the last time coordinating the group was a pain. Capture their exact words, and mark which of our frustrations they share.
3. Note who we can actually get on a call this week, and what people already pay for or hack around today.
4. Come with a first stab at the issue in one sentence, plus 3 to 4 candidate use cases.

The bar: no opinion without a data point behind it, and we're the first data point. If we each show up having talked to even one real person, Thursday is a decision instead of a brainstorm.

Full framework is in the planning docs if you want to go deeper. Looking forward to it, see you Thursday.

Odi
