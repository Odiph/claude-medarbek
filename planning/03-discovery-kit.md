# Discovery Kit — How to Bring Real Problem Evidence

The gating first step. Ship this before anyone dials. It has three parts:
1. **The mini-lesson** — 10 min, so the calls don't lead the witness
2. **The interview guide** — what to actually ask
3. **The evidence log** — where the data lands, in a comparable shape

The only goal of a discovery call: come back with **evidence about the problem**, not a reaction to our idea.

---

## Part 1 — Mini-lesson (read before your first call)

### The one rule
**Ask about their past, not your future.** People are honest about what they *did* and terrible at predicting what they *would* do. "Would you use a widget for HOA events?" gets you a polite yes. "Walk me through the last event you organized" gets you the truth.

### Evidence vs. opinion — what counts
| Counts as evidence ✅ | Doesn't count ❌ |
|----------------------|------------------|
| A specific past event ("last month I spent 3 hours chasing dues") | A hypothetical ("I'd probably use that") |
| Something they already pay for or built a workaround around | A compliment on our idea ("cool concept") |
| An emotional spike — frustration, a workaround they're proud of | A feature request ("it should have X") |
| A number they say unprompted (time, $, frequency) | Our paraphrase of what they "basically meant" |

If your notes are full of the right column, the call failed. Redo the next one.

### The four traps
- **Leading:** "Don't you hate when…?" → they agree to be nice. Ask "how do you handle…?" instead.
- **Pitching:** the moment you explain the product, discovery is over. Save it for the last 2 min.
- **Hypotheticals:** "would you / could you / do you usually" → swap for "when was the last time you…"
- **Accepting compliments:** "love it!" is not data. Dig: "when did you last hit that problem?"

### What you're mining for
Signals the pain is **real, frequent, costly, and reachable** — the four axes we score on Thursday. Every question below aims at one of them.

## Part 2 — Interview guide (20 min)

Print this. Take verbatim notes — exact words, not summaries.

**Opening (1 min)**
> "I'm not selling anything — we're trying to understand how [HOA managers] handle [community operations] today. There are no wrong answers; I just want to hear how it actually works for you. Mind if I take notes?"

**Context (3 min)** — who they are
- "Walk me through your role — what are you responsible for day to day?"
- "How big is the community / how many people do you deal with?"

**The problem story (10 min)** — the heart of it
- "Tell me about the last time [organizing an event / collecting dues / getting the community to show up] was a pain. What happened?"
- "Walk me through exactly what you did — step by step."
- "How often does that come up?" *(frequency)*
- "What did that cost you — time, money, headache?" *(cost)*
- "What do you use to handle it today?" *(current solution / substitute)*
- "What have you tried that didn't work?"
- *(If they mention paying for something)* "What do you pay for that? Is it worth it?" *(willingness to pay)*

**The tell (3 min)** — intensity
- "On that last bad one — how big a deal was it, honestly?"
- "If it magically disappeared tomorrow, what would change for you?"

**Reachability + referral (2 min)**
- "Who else deals with this that I should talk to?"
- "Can I follow up if I have a couple more questions?"

**Only now, the last 1–2 min** — if they ask what you're building, describe it in one line and watch the reaction. Note it, but it's the weakest evidence in the call.

## Part 3 — Evidence log template

One row per person you talk to. Fill from verbatim notes. This is the sheet we score from on Thursday.

| # | Who (role / customer type) | Pain, in their words (quote) | How often | What it costs them | What they do today | $ signal (pays / would pay / none) | Reachable? (easy/hard) | Interviewer |

### Fill rules
- **Quotes, not paraphrases.** If you can't put it in quotes, you didn't hear it — leave it blank.
- **Blank is a finding.** An empty "$ signal" column across five rows means no one feels this enough to pay. That's a real, useful answer.
- **Tag the customer type** so we can compare HOA vs. an alternative candidate side by side.

---

## Definition of done for the kit
- [ ] Everyone read Part 1 before their first call
- [ ] Guide (Part 2) in hand on each call
- [ ] Evidence log has ≥5 rows, mostly quotes, across ≥2 customer types
- [ ] At least one row with a real $ signal — or we note that none exists and treat that as the headline finding
