# Homework — Before Thursday

Each person brings inputs for both goals so the session is a decision, not a brainstorm. **Talk to a real person before you walk in** — one conversation beats an hour of opinion.

---

## Tejas

**For Goal 1 (is the issue real?)**
- **2 discovery touches** with HOA/community people. Not a pitch — "walk me through the last time [running the community] was a pain." Bring **verbatim quotes**: what hurt, how often, what it cost, what they do today.
- **Reachability check:** list who you can actually get on a call this week. If you can't reach ~5, say so — that alone tells us whether the issue passes the "Real" test.

**For Goal 2 (which use case?)**
- Note the **specific situations** where the pain showed up ("collecting dues," "getting people to the event"). Those become candidate use cases.

---

## Jason

**For Goal 1 (is the issue real?)**
- **1 discovery touch** (different contact than Tejas — spread the angle).
- **Pull existing signal:** waitlist, past conversations, inbound — which pain do people raise *unprompted*?
- **What do they pay for today?** Any current spend or workaround = hardest evidence the issue is real and costly.

**For Goal 2 (which use case?)**
- Bring **2–3 candidate use cases** you think live inside the issue, each as *one actor / one job / one trigger* (not "engage members" — too broad).

---

## Both bring, in one sentence each
1. **The issue** as you'd state it: *"[customer] repeatedly struggles to [job] because [friction], which costs them [x]."*
2. **Your top use-case guess** inside it: *"when [actor] hits [trigger], they need [job]."*

Bring these **independently** — we compare, we don't pre-align. Where you disagree is where the real discussion is.

---

## What "done" looks like
- [ ] 3 real conversations total (2 Tejas, 1 Jason), captured as quotes not summaries
- [ ] Reachability list — or the gap named
- [ ] Existing signal + any current spend pulled (Jason)
- [ ] Each of you: one written issue statement + one use-case guess

> The bar: no opinion without at least one data point behind it. If nobody talked to a customer, Thursday slips to gathering data instead of deciding.
